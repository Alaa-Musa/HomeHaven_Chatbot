## Overview
This document outlines enhancements to the in-store experience across all HomeHaven locations. The goal is to create a more personalised, efficient, and accessible shopping environment that drives higher satisfaction, faster service, and increased loyalty. The enhancements cover staff training, guest journey mapping, accessibility upgrades, and measurement of impact.

## Scope
- Applies to all physical stores in UAE and any pop-up events hosted by HomeHaven.
- Impacts floor staff, supervisors, and store managers.
- Works in tandem with Click & Collect and online support.

## Staff Training & Knowledge
- Launch a quarterly training cadence focusing on product familiarity, upsell techniques, and customer empathy.
- Introduce micro-learning modules (5–7 minutes) embedded in POS terminals and the store app.
- Create a rotating “product specialist of the week” program to deepen expertise on a subset of popular items (e.g., AP-400, SN-L2, DW60).
- Implement live role-play sessions to rehearse common customer scenarios (returns, exchanges, warranty claims).

## Guest Experience Protocols
- Greeting standard: “Welcome to HomeHaven—how can I help you today?” within 15 seconds of entry.
- Needs discovery: staff ask 2–3 open-ended questions to identify the customer’s goal.
- Demo zones: create small, clearly marked product demonstration areas with tactile displays.
- Checkout flow: minimize queue time via a dedicated express line for simple purchases.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
- Ensure accessible routes and signage with clear font sizes, high contrast, and tactile guides where necessary.
- Wheelchair-friendly aisles with 1100 mm of clear width; seating at product demo areas.
- Hearing-impaired accommodation equipment (visual announcements, captioned video loops).

## Returns Processing In-Store
- Staff are trained to verify packaging integrity and accessories with customers cooperating on the spot.
- For in-store returns, initiate the same condition checks as courier returns (HH-21) and issue refunds or store credits per policy.

## Store Experience Metrics
- Customer satisfaction (CSAT) targets: 4.6/5 on average; Net Promoter Score (NPS) targets: >70 quarterly.
- First-contact resolution (FCR) target: 85% on in-store inquiries.
- Throughput: target average time in-store from entry to exit under 6 minutes for routine purchases.

## Change Management
- Pilot new experience in 3 locations for 90 days, then roll out to all stores.
- Collect qualitative feedback from customers and frontline teams to adjust training content.
