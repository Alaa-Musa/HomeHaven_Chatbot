# Internal Audit Policy  


## 1. Purpose
The purpose of the Internal Audit Policy is to establish an independent and objective internal audit function that evaluates and improves the effectiveness of risk management, internal controls, and governance processes within the company.

The Internal Audit function provides assurance to management and the board that company operations, financial reporting, inventory management, and compliance activities are operating effectively.

---

## 2. Scope
The Internal Audit function covers all departments and activities within the company, including but not limited to:

- Retail store operations
- Inventory and warehouse management
- Procurement and supplier management
- Sales and point-of-sale (POS) transactions
- Finance and accounting
- IT systems and data security
- Customer service operations
- Compliance with company policies and regulatory requirements

---

## 3. Objectives of Internal Audit
The objectives of the Internal Audit function include:

1. Assess the effectiveness of internal controls.
2. Evaluate risk management processes.
3. Ensure compliance with company policies and procedures.
4. Verify accuracy and reliability of financial information.
5. Detect and prevent fraud or operational irregularities.
6. Improve operational efficiency and cost control.
7. Safeguard company assets including inventory and cash.

---

## 4. Independence
The Internal Audit Department must operate independently from operational management.

- The **Head of Internal Audit** reports functionally to the **Audit Committee / Board of Directors**.
- Administratively, the Head of Internal Audit may report to the **Chief Executive Officer (CEO)**.
- Internal auditors must not audit activities they are directly responsible for.

---

## 5. Internal Audit Responsibilities
The Internal Audit Department is responsible for:

- Developing an **annual risk-based audit plan**
- Conducting operational, financial, and compliance audits
- Evaluating internal controls across all departments
- Investigating suspected fraud or irregularities
- Reporting audit findings and recommendations to management
- Monitoring implementation of corrective actions

---

## 6. Audit Planning
An **Annual Audit Plan** shall be prepared based on risk assessment and approved by the Audit Committee.

Factors considered in risk assessment include:

- High-value inventory
- Cash handling activities
- High transaction volumes
- New store openings
- New IT systems
- Prior audit findings
- Regulatory requirements

---

## 7. Types of Internal Audits
The Internal Audit Department may perform the following types of audits:

### Operational Audit
Review efficiency of store operations, logistics, procurement, and customer service.

### Financial Audit
Review financial records, accounting accuracy, and compliance with accounting standards.

### Compliance Audit
Ensure adherence to company policies and regulatory requirements.

### Inventory Audit
Verify physical stock against system records to detect discrepancies.

### IT Audit
Assess IT security, POS systems, ERP systems, and data protection controls.

### Fraud Investigation
Investigate suspected fraud, theft, or financial irregularities.

---

## 8. Audit Procedures
The standard internal audit process includes:

1. Audit planning
2. Risk assessment
3. Fieldwork and data collection
4. Testing of internal controls
5. Identification of findings
6. Preparation of audit report
7. Discussion with management
8. Follow-up on corrective actions

---

## 9. Reporting
Internal Audit reports must include:

- Audit objective
- Scope of review
- Key findings
- Risk level (High / Medium / Low)
- Recommendations
- Management responses
- Implementation deadlines

Reports shall be submitted to:

- Senior Management
- Audit Committee
- Board of Directors (where applicable)

---

## 10. Access to Information
Internal auditors shall have **unrestricted access** to:

- Company records
- Financial documents
- Inventory systems
- POS transaction records
- Staff and management

All employees must cooperate with internal audit activities.

---

## 11. Confidentiality
Internal auditors must maintain strict confidentiality regarding company information, audit findings, and sensitive financial data.

---

## 12. Follow-Up
Internal Audit will monitor the implementation of corrective actions.

Follow-up reviews will be conducted to ensure:

- Recommendations are implemented
- Risks are mitigated
- Controls are strengthened

---

## 13. Review of Policy
This policy shall be reviewed **annually** or whenever significant changes occur in the business operations.
