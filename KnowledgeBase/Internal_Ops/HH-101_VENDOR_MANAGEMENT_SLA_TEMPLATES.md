## Purpose
This document provides guidelines for creating Service Level Agreements (SLAs) with vendors. SLAs help define expectations for performance, quality, and accountability, ensuring reliable service delivery for home retail operations.

---

## Scope
This policy applies to:

- All vendors providing goods or services to the company
- Logistics, supply chain, and procurement vendors
- Technology and IT service providers
- Maintenance, repair, and professional services vendors

---

## Objectives
- Establish clear performance expectations with vendors.
- Ensure timely delivery of goods and services.
- Maintain consistent product or service quality.
- Provide mechanisms for monitoring, reporting, and escalation of issues.

---

## Key SLA Components

### 1. Response Times
- Define maximum response times for vendor queries, support requests, or issue resolution.
- Specify different response levels based on severity:
  - Critical issues: immediate or within 2 hours
  - High-priority issues: within 24 hours
  - Standard issues: within 3–5 business days

### 2. Delivery Windows
- Define delivery timelines for products or services.
- Include acceptable tolerance levels for early or late deliveries.
- Specify responsibilities for delays and corrective actions.

### 3. Quality Metrics
- Outline measurable quality standards for goods or services.
- Examples include:
  - Product defect rates
  - Accuracy of deliveries
  - Compliance with packaging and labeling standards
  - Service uptime or performance metrics for IT vendors

### 4. Monitoring and Reporting
- Require vendors to provide periodic performance reports.
- Monitor compliance against SLA metrics regularly.
- Use dashboards or scorecards to track vendor performance.

### 5. Escalation Paths
- Define escalation procedures for SLA breaches.
- Specify contacts and hierarchy for issue resolution.
- Include timelines and actions for escalation to senior management if needed.

### 6. Review and Continuous Improvement
- Conduct regular performance reviews with vendors.
- Adjust SLAs based on evolving business needs and historical performance.
- Encourage vendors to propose process improvements and efficiencies.

---

## Roles and Responsibilities

### Procurement / Vendor Management Team
- Draft and negotiate SLA terms with vendors.
- Monitor vendor performance against agreed metrics.
- Maintain records of SLAs and performance reports.

### Vendors
- Adhere to SLA terms, response times, and quality standards.
- Submit required reports and documentation on time.
- Notify the company promptly of any potential issues impacting SLA compliance.

### Operations / Store Teams
- Provide feedback on vendor performance.
- Report any SLA breaches or recurring issues to vendor management.

---

## Policy Review
- SLAs should be reviewed annually or whenever significant changes occur in vendor scope, business requirements, or operational priorities.
- Ensure alignment with company objectives and regulatory requirements.
