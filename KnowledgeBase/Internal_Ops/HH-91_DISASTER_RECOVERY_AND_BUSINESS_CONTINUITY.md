# Disaster Recovery and Business Continuity Policy

## Objectives
- Maintain essential business operations during disruptions such as natural disasters, IT system failures, supply chain interruptions, or facility outages.
- Protect customer, employee, and company data from loss or compromise.
- Ensure the safety and well-being of employees and customers.
- Minimize financial loss and operational downtime.
- Restore normal operations as quickly and efficiently as possible.

---

## Scope
This policy applies to all business units, retail stores, warehouses, IT systems, and employees of the company. It covers disruptions affecting:

- Retail store operations
- Warehouse and logistics operations
- Information technology systems
- Financial and payment systems
- Customer service operations
- Corporate offices

---

## Key Plans

### Data Backup and Recovery
- Critical business data must be backed up regularly (daily or as defined by IT policy).
- Backups should be stored in secure offsite or cloud locations.
- Data recovery procedures must be documented and tested periodically.

### IT System Recovery
- Maintain disaster recovery procedures for key systems such as POS, ERP, inventory management, and financial systems.
- Establish recovery time objectives (RTO) and recovery point objectives (RPO).
- Ensure redundancy for critical infrastructure where feasible.

### Alternate Communication Channels
- Maintain emergency communication channels for employees and management.
- Use multiple methods such as email, messaging systems, phone trees, or internal communication platforms.
- Ensure management can quickly communicate operational updates during disruptions.

### Contingency Staffing
- Identify backup personnel for critical roles.
- Cross-train employees where possible to support key operations during emergencies.
- Develop plans for temporary staffing or redistribution of staff across locations.

### Alternative Operational Arrangements
- Establish procedures for operating from alternate locations if primary facilities are unavailable.
- Enable remote work capabilities for applicable roles where possible.

---

## Emergency Response
In the event of a disaster or major disruption:

1. Ensure immediate safety of employees and customers.
2. Notify management and activate the Business Continuity Plan.
3. Assess operational impact and prioritize critical functions.
4. Initiate disaster recovery procedures for affected systems.
5. Communicate updates to employees, stakeholders, and customers as necessary.

---

## Roles and Responsibilities

### Management
- Ensure implementation and oversight of the business continuity and disaster recovery plans.
- Allocate resources for risk mitigation and recovery.

### IT Department
- Maintain data backups and disaster recovery systems.
- Test system recovery procedures regularly.

### Store and Operations Managers
- Implement emergency procedures at retail locations.
- Ensure staff are aware of safety protocols and communication procedures.

### Employees
- Follow emergency procedures and cooperate during recovery operations.

---

## Testing and Training
- Conduct regular disaster recovery and business continuity drills.
- Train employees on emergency response procedures.
- Review and test data restoration processes periodically.

---

## Plan Review and Updates
The Disaster Recovery and Business Continuity Plan must be reviewed at least annually or after any major operational change or disruption to ensure effectiveness and relevance.
