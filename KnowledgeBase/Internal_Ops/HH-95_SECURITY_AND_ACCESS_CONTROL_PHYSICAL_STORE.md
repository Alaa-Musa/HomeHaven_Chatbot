## Purpose
This document outlines the security and access control measures required to protect store assets, employees, customers, and sensitive operational areas within physical retail locations.

---

## Scope
This policy applies to all physical retail locations, including:

- Sales floor
- Inventory storage rooms
- Cash handling areas
- Offices and administrative areas
- Loading docks and receiving areas

---

## Objectives
- Protect company assets including inventory, equipment, and cash.
- Ensure the safety of employees and customers.
- Prevent unauthorized access to restricted areas.
- Support investigation and monitoring through surveillance systems.

---

## Access Control

### Controlled Entry Points
- Staff entry points must be clearly designated and monitored.
- Employees must use authorized access credentials (e.g., keycards, access codes, or physical keys).
- Access permissions should be granted based on job responsibilities.

### Visitor Management
- Visitors entering restricted areas must sign a visitor log.
- Visitors must be accompanied by authorized staff when entering non-public areas.
- Temporary access badges may be issued where applicable.

---

## Restricted Areas

### Inventory Rooms
- Inventory storage areas must remain locked when not actively in use.
- Access should be limited to authorized staff such as inventory managers, supervisors, and store management.
- Inventory movements should be documented in the inventory management system.

### Cash Handling Areas
- Cash offices or safes must be secured with restricted access.
- Only authorized employees responsible for cash management may enter these areas.
- Cash counts and transfers must follow established financial control procedures.

---

## Surveillance and Monitoring

### Camera Coverage
- Security cameras should monitor public areas including:
  - Sales floor
  - Store entrances and exits
  - Checkout counters
  - Inventory receiving areas

### Data Retention
- Video recordings should be stored for a defined retention period in accordance with company policy and local regulations.
- Access to recorded footage should be limited to authorized personnel.

---

## Alarm and Security Systems
- Stores should be equipped with alarm systems to detect unauthorized entry after business hours.
- Alarm systems must be activated when the store is closed.
- Only designated personnel should have alarm system access codes.

---

## Incident Reporting
All security incidents must be reported immediately to store management.

Examples include:
- Unauthorized access attempts
- Theft or suspected theft
- Security system failures
- Suspicious behavior

An incident report should be completed and reviewed by management for further action.

---

## Employee Responsibilities
All employees are responsible for maintaining store security by:

- Following access control procedures
- Reporting security concerns promptly
- Protecting company property
- Ensuring restricted areas remain secured

---

## Policy Review
This security and access control policy should be reviewed periodically to ensure it remains effective and aligned with company operations and security requirements.
