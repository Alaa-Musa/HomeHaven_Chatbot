## Purpose
This policy outlines the procedures for handling returned items in a home retail environment, ensuring that products are safely repackaged, inspected, and made available for resale or reuse while maintaining quality and customer satisfaction.

---

## Scope
This policy applies to:

- Returned products from customers
- Items returned from stores or warehouses
- All product categories, including furniture, home décor, appliances, and accessories
- Staff involved in returns processing, quality inspection, and repackaging

---

## Objectives
- Ensure returned items are repackaged safely and securely for resale or redistribution.
- Maintain product quality and brand reputation.
- Optimize reuse and minimize waste.
- Document returns and repacking procedures for traceability.

---

## Repacking Guidelines

### 1. Item Inspection
- Inspect all returned items for damage, missing parts, and overall condition.
- Classify items based on condition:
  - **Grade A:** Like new, fully functional, resalable without repair.
  - **Grade B:** Minor wear or cosmetic defects, functional, suitable for resale with minor adjustments.
  - **Grade C:** Damaged, incomplete, or non-functional; not suitable for resale.

### 2. Repackaging Process
- Use original packaging whenever possible.
- Include all original accessories, manuals, warranty cards, and documentation.
- Replace damaged packaging materials with equivalent protective packaging if necessary.
- Ensure items are securely packed to prevent damage during storage or transport.

### 3. Labeling
- Clearly label repackaged items with:
  - Condition grade (A/B/C)
  - Date of repackaging
  - SKU or product code
  - Any notable defects (for Grade B items)
- Maintain records of repackaged items for inventory and audit purposes.

### 4. Storage and Handling
- Store repackaged items in designated areas based on grade:
  - Grade A items ready for immediate resale
  - Grade B items may require discounting or special handling
  - Grade C items should be directed to repair, recycling, or disposal
- Handle items with care to prevent additional damage during movement and storage.

---

## Reuse and Redistribution
- Evaluate opportunities to reuse returned items internally or donate to charitable organizations if resale is not possible.
- Repair or refurbish items when feasible to maximize product lifecycle.
- Document all reuse, repair, or donation activities for compliance and traceability.

---

## Responsibilities
- **Returns Team:** Inspect, classify, and repack returned items.
- **Inventory/Warehouse Team:** Store repackaged items according to guidelines and maintain accurate inventory records.
- **Store/Operations Managers:** Ensure staff follow repacking and reuse procedures consistently.

---

## Policy Review
This policy should be reviewed at least annually or whenever significant changes occur in return processes, packaging materials, or product types.
