## Escalation Tiers
- Tier 1: Frontline support
- Tier 2: Specialist teams
- Tier 3: Management and legal/compliance

Overview
To ensure timely resolution of customer issues, this matrix defines the workflow for moving inquiries through progressive levels of authority.
2. Escalation TiersTier 1: Frontline Support

* Personnel: Customer Service Associates (Phone, Chat, Email).
* Scope: General inquiries, order tracking, standard returns, and basic product information.
* Authority: Standard discount codes (up to 10%), replacement of damaged parts, and processing returns within the 30-day window.
* SLA: Immediate response; resolution within 24 hours.

Tier 2: Specialist Teams

* Personnel: Subject Matter Experts (SMEs), Floor Leads, or Billing Specialists.
* Scope: Complex shipping logistics (e.g., white-glove delivery failures), technical assembly issues, and recurring billing discrepancies.
* Authority: Refunds outside standard windows, goodwill gestures (up to $100), and coordination with third-party logistics (3PL) partners.
* SLA: Response within 4 business hours; resolution within 48 hours.

Tier 3: Management & Compliance

* Personnel: Department Managers, Legal Counsel, or Executive Resolution Team.
* Scope: High-value claims ($2,000+), threats of litigation, property damage during delivery, and social media crises.
* Authority: Full liability settlements, legal responses, and structural policy overrides.
* SLA: Response within 24 business hours; resolution timeline determined by case complexity.

3. Trigger Criteria
Issues must be escalated immediately if:

* A customer explicitly requests a Supervisor.
* The issue involves safety hazards or product recalls.
* Legal action or "Better Business Bureau" (BBB) reports are mentioned.
