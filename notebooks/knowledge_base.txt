*   **Payment Speed:** Orders paid via Credit Card or Apple Pay are verified instantly. **Cash on Delivery (COD)** orders may require a brief phone verification, which could push your order past the cut-off if you are unreachable.

# AirPure AP-400 — Filter Replacement Steps (HH-46)

## 1. Overview
To maintain the Air Quality Index (AQI) standards of your HomeHaven, the **AirPure AP-400** requires periodic filter replacement. Timely maintenance prevents motor strain and ensures that 99.97% of desert dust and household allergens are effectively trapped.

## 2. Identification: When to Replace
While the AP-400 features an intelligent "Filter Reset" indicator light, you should perform a manual inspection if you notice any of the following:
*   **Persistent Odors:** The activated carbon layer is saturated and can no longer neutralize cooking or pet smells.
*   **Reduced Airflow:** The HEPA-style core is clogged with fine particulate matter, causing the fan to sound louder while pushing less air.
*   **Visual Discoloration:** The outer pre-filter has turned dark grey or black despite regular vacuuming.
*   **Static Indicator:** The filter light remains illuminated even after a basic cleaning of the external grilles.

## 3. Step-by-Step Installation Guide

### Step 1: Safety & Preparation
*   **Power Down:** Press the Power button to turn off the unit.
*   **Disconnect:** Unplug the power cord from the UAE wall socket. Never attempt a filter change while the unit is connected to electricity.

### Step 2: Accessing the Core
*   **Positioning:** Lay the unit on its side on a soft surface (like a rug) or turn it upside down.
*   **Compartment:** Locate the filter hatch at the base. Twist the locking knob counter-clockwise to release the safety cover.

### Step 3: Extraction & Cleaning
*   **Removal:** Pull the old filter pack out by the fabric tabs. 
*   **Disposal:** Place the old filter in a sealed bag immediately to prevent trapped dust from re-entering your room.
*   **Internal Wipe:** Use a dry microfiber cloth to wipe the internal sensor lens and the interior walls of the filter chamber.

### Step 4: Installing the New AP-F400 Pack
*   **Packaging Warning:** **Crucial Step.** Remove all plastic wrapping from the new **AP-F400** filter. Failure to do so will cause the motor to overheat and potentially fail within minutes.
*   **Insertion:** Slide the new filter into the chamber with the tabs facing outward. Ensure it is seated flush against the internal gaskets.

### Step 5: Finalizing & Reset
*   **Closure:** Replace the bottom cover and twist the knob clockwise until it clicks into the locked position.
*   **Power On:** Plug the unit back in and press Power.
*   **System Reset:** Press and hold the "Filter" or "Sleep" button (depending on your model revision) for **3–5 seconds** until the indicator light turns off or beeps.

## 4. Verification
Run the unit on "Turbo" mode for 5 minutes. Check the exhaust grille at the top to confirm strong, clean-smelling airflow and ensure the laser particle sensor is updating the LED color ring accurately.

## 5. Maintenance Pro-Tip
To extend the life of your new **AP-F400** pack, vacuum the outermost mesh layer of the filter every 2 weeks to remove large lint and pet hair. This allows the inner HEPA layer to focus solely on microscopic particles.

# DustDash DD-VX2 — Maintenance Schedule (HH-47)

## 1. Overview
To maintain the high-velocity suction and 35-minute runtime of your **DustDash DD-VX2**, a consistent maintenance routine is essential. Given the fine nature of desert dust in the UAE, these steps prevent motor overheating and ensure the longevity of the brushless digital system.

## 2. Weekly Maintenance: Performance Optimization
Perform these quick checks every 3–4 uses to prevent mechanical strain:
*   **Dust Cup Clearance:** Empty the 0.6L dust bin once it reaches the "MAX" line. Operating a full bin reduces cyclonic separation efficiency and pushes dust into the secondary filters.
*   **Intake Inspection:** Check the "V-Shape" floor head and the extension wand for blockages. Use a long, non-sharp object to clear any trapped debris (like large tissue fragments or plastic).
*   **Brush Roll Detangling:** Hair and threads can wrap around the motorized brush. Use a pair of scissors to carefully snip along the designated groove on the roller to release hair and prevent bearing seize.

## 3. Monthly Maintenance: Deep Filtration Care
The DD-VX2 relies on its multi-stage HEPA-style filtration to maintain clean exhaust air.
*   **Filter Rinse:** Remove the HEPA-style filter and rinse it under cold, low-pressure running water. Do not use soap, detergents, or a washing machine.
*   **The 24-Hour Rule:** **Crucial Step.** Allow the filter to air-dry completely for at least **24 hours** in a well-ventilated area (away from direct UAE sunlight). 
*   **Warning:** Reinserting a damp or even slightly humid filter will cause irreversible motor damage and potentially create a persistent musty odor. This is considered "misuse" and is not covered under the standard warranty (HH-25).

## 4. Troubleshooting Performance Drops
If you notice a decrease in suction power or a shorter-than-expected battery runtime:
*   **Mode Check:** Ensure the unit isn't accidentally locked in "Boost Mode" for hard floors. Switching to **Eco Mode** provides the optimal balance of power and a full 35-minute runtime for tiled surfaces.
*   **Filter Integrity:** Inspect the filter for "caking." If the fine mesh is greyed out despite washing, the filter has reached its capacity. Replace it with a genuine **DD-FLT2** pack.
*   **Battery Calibration:** If the runtime is inconsistent, perform a full discharge followed by an uninterrupted 5-hour charge cycle to recalibrate the Lithium-Ion cells.

## 5. Annual Checklist
*   **Battery Replacement:** If the total runtime in Eco mode drops below 15 minutes after 12 months of daily use, consider purchasing a replacement **DD-BAT2** battery.
*   **Seal Inspection:** Check the rubber gaskets around the dust cup lid. If they appear dry or cracked, wipe them with a tiny amount of silicone grease to maintain the airtight vacuum seal.

# SteamShine SS-M10 — Floor Safety Notes (HH-48)

## 1. Overview
The **SteamShine SS-M10** uses high-temperature pressurized steam (110°C) to sanitize and lift grime. Because heat and moisture can affect different materials uniquely, adhering to these safety notes is essential to prevent warping, clouding, or permanent damage to your home’s flooring.

## 2. Approved & Safe Surfaces
The SS-M10 is optimized for hard, non-porous surfaces that are professionally sealed:
*   **Ceramic & Porcelain Tiles:** Ideal for high-heat steam; excellent for cleaning grout lines.
*   **Sealed Hardwood:** Safe only if the finish is intact with no visible gaps between planks. Use the "Low Steam" setting if available.
*   **Sealed Laminate:** Use with caution. Ensure the laminate is rated for steam cleaning by the manufacturer; excessive moisture can cause "peaking" at the joints.
*   **Polished Stone (Marble/Granite):** Safe for sealed stone. High-frequency use on unsealed stone may dull the natural luster over time.

## 3. Surfaces to Avoid (High Risk)
Do not use the SS-M10 on the following surfaces, as steam can cause immediate and irreversible damage:
*   **Unsealed Wood:** Steam will penetrate the wood fibers, causing them to swell, warp, or grow mold.
*   **Waxed or Polished Floors:** The high temperature will melt the wax or polish, leaving a cloudy, sticky residue and stripping the protective layer.
*   **Luxury Vinyl Planks (LVP) / Vinyl Tiles:** Some vinyl adhesives are heat-sensitive; check your flooring warranty before use as steam may cause the planks to lift.
*   **Delicate Surfaces:** Silk rugs, velvet upholstery, or any "Dry Clean Only" fabrics.

## 4. Professional Testing Protocol
*   **The "Hidden Corner" Test:** Before cleaning a visible area, apply steam to a small, inconspicuous section (e.g., behind a door or under a sofa) for **60 seconds**. 
*   **Post-Test Inspection:** Wait for the area to dry completely. Check for any swelling, discoloration, loss of shine, or texture changes before proceeding with the rest of the room.

## 5. Operational Safety Tips
*   **Continuous Motion:** Never leave the steam mop stationary in one spot while the steam is engaged. Constant heat concentration in a single area can "burn" or cloud the floor finish.
*   **Pad Saturation:** Once the microfiber pad is fully saturated with water and dirt, replace it with a fresh one. A soaking wet pad can leave excess moisture in floor joints.
*   **Cool Down:** After use, remove the damp pad immediately and allow the mop head to cool on a non-heat-sensitive surface (like a tiled balcony or laundry room floor).

# CrispWave CW-AF55 — Quick Cooking Tips (HH-49)

## 1. Overview

## 2. Professional Cooking Techniques
*   **Preheating Protocol:** Always preheat the unit at 200°C for 3–5 minutes. A hot basket ensures immediate searing of proteins and superior "crisp" on frozen goods.
*   **Aeration Spacing:** Avoid overfilling the basket. For meat and poultry, maintain a single layer with at least 1cm of space between items to allow unobstructed high-velocity airflow.
*   **The "Mid-Cycle Shake":** For fries, nuggets, or roasted vegetables, remove and shake the basket halfway through the timer. This redistributes oils and prevents "soft spots" caused by steam trapped between layers.
*   **Dry to Fry:** Pat proteins (like chicken skin or fish) completely dry with a paper towel before seasoning. Any surface moisture will create steam instead of a crisp crust.

| **Vegetables** | 190°C | 8–10 mins | Toss in a bowl with seasoning before adding to the basket. |
| **Frozen Snacks** | 200°C | 6–10 mins | No extra oil required. |

*   **External Clearances:** Maintain at least 15cm of open space behind the exhaust vent during operation to prevent overheating and wall discoloration.

# PureBlend PB-900 — Safety & Overheating Prevention (HH-50)

## 1. Overview
The **PureBlend PB-900** features a high-torque 900W motor designed for rapid emulsification. Due to its high-speed operation, strict adherence to safety and thermal management protocols is required to prevent motor burnout and ensure user safety in the kitchen.

## 2. Safety Reminders
*   **Dual-Lock Lid Security:** Ensure the lid is pressed down firmly and the locking tabs are engaged. The PB-900 utilizes a safety interlock; the blades will not spin unless the sensor detects a seated lid.
*   **Thermal Expansion Warning:** **Do not blend boiling or carbonated liquids.** Steam buildup creates extreme internal pressure, which can cause the lid to eject or the pitcher to crack, leading to severe burn injuries.
*   **Mechanical Safety:** Keep hands, hair, and kitchen utensils (spatulas, spoons) away from the pitcher while the motor is engaged. Only use the provided tamper through the lid opening if the motor is at a complete stop.
*   **Stable Base:** Ensure the suction-cup feet are secured to a dry, flat surface before operation to prevent "walking" during high-speed cycles.

## 3. Overheating Prevention & Duty Cycle
To protect the motor’s internal copper windings and extend the life of the appliance, follow the **60/120 Rule**:

*   **Maximum Run Time:** Do not run the blender continuously for more than **60 seconds**.
*   **Mandatory Cooling Interval:** Allow the motor to rest for at least **2 minutes** between cycles.
*   **Heavy Loads:** When processing thick mixtures (e.g., nut butters or frozen açaí), reduce the continuous run time to **30 seconds** to prevent the Thermal Overload Protector (TOP) from tripping.
*   **Tripped Reset:** If the unit shuts off automatically, unplug it immediately. Wait **30 minutes** for the internal components to cool before pressing the "Reset" button located on the base.

## 4. Operational Best Practices
*   **Liquid-First Loading:** To reduce motor strain, always add liquid ingredients first, followed by soft solids, and finish with ice or frozen items on top.
*   **Pulse vs. Continuous:** Use the "Pulse" function for initial breakdown of large chunks. This prevents the blades from jamming and reduces the initial torque load on the motor.
*   **Blade Inspection:** Check the underside of the blade assembly regularly for signs of wear or leakage. If the bearing becomes stiff, stop use immediately to avoid overheating the drive socket.

# Cash on Delivery — FAQs (HH-60)

## 1. Overview
While HomeHaven encourages digital payments for a contactless experience, we offer **Cash on Delivery (COD)** to provide flexibility for our UAE customers. This document addresses common queries regarding the handling of physical currency during the delivery process.

## 2. Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is Cash on Delivery available everywhere?**  
A: COD is available for most residential areas across the UAE. However, it may be restricted based on:
*   **Order Value:** Maximum limit of **AED 5,000** per transaction.
*   **Location:** Certain remote or "off-shore" locations may require pre-payment.
*   **Service Fee:** A non-refundable convenience fee of **AED 15** is applied to all COD orders to cover additional logistics handling.

**Q: Can I pay by card at the door?**  
A: **Not guaranteed.** While some of our last-mile partners carry mobile POS terminals, network connectivity and hardware availability vary by route. To ensure a successful transaction via Credit/Debit card, we strongly recommend paying at the online checkout.

**Q: Should I prepare exact change?**  
A: **Yes.** Our couriers carry limited "float" for security reasons. Providing the exact amount in UAE Dirhams (AED) significantly speeds up the delivery process and ensures the driver can move to the next scheduled stop on time.

**Q: Can I open the box before paying the courier?**  
A: No. Our "Open-Box" policy only applies after the payment is collected. If the item is found to be damaged or incorrect after payment, please follow the **Standard Return Process (HH-52)** for a full refund or exchange.

**Q: What happens if I don't have the cash ready when the driver arrives?**  
A: The driver will wait for a maximum of 10 minutes. If payment cannot be made, the delivery will be marked as "Failed" and returned to the hub. A re-delivery fee may apply for the second attempt.

## 3. Refund Policy for COD
*   **Returns:** Refunds for items purchased via COD are issued via **Bank Transfer** (7-10 days) or **HomeHaven Store Credit** (Instant). 
*   **Cash Refunds:** Under no circumstances will our drivers or retail stores issue physical cash refunds for online COD orders.

# HavenPlus Rewards — FAQs (HH-61)

## 1. Overview
**HavenPlus** is the official loyalty program for HomeHaven UAE. Members earn points on every qualifying purchase, which can be converted into significant savings on future household essentials and smart-home upgrades.

## 2. Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: When do I receive my HavenPlus points?**  
A: Points are automatically credited to your "Member Dashboard" once an order is officially marked as **Delivered** (for courier shipments) or **Picked Up** (for Click & Collect). For in-store purchases, points are updated within 2 hours of the transaction.

**Q: How do I redeem my points for discounts?**  
A: You can convert points into "HomeHaven Credit" at the online checkout or via the mobile app. 
*   **Conversion Rate:** **500 points = AED 25** discount. 
*   **Incremental Rule:** Redemptions must be made in fixed increments of 500 (e.g., 500, 1000, 1500 points).

**Q: Do HavenPlus points expire?**  
A: **Yes.** Points are valid for **12 months** from the date they were earned. We operate on a "Rolling Expiry" basis, meaning only the points earned in a specific month will expire a year later, rather than the entire balance. 

**Q: Do I earn points on every part of my order?**  
A: Points are earned on the net product value (after discounts). You **do not** earn points on:
*   Standard or Scheduled Delivery fees.
*   Professional Installation charges.
*   Cash on Delivery (COD) service fees.
*   Extended Warranty plan purchases.

**Q: Can I share my points with friends or family?**  
A: No. Points are non-transferable and are tied to the unique Email/Mobile Number used at registration.

**Q: What happens to my points if I return an item?**  
A: If a product is returned for a refund, the points earned from that specific item will be automatically deducted from your HavenPlus balance. If your balance is zero, the equivalent value (AED 0.05 per point) will be deducted from your refund total.

## 3. Membership Tiers
*   **Silver:** Entry level (0–5,000 points annually).
*   **Gold:** 1.5x points multiplier + Early access to Seasonal Sales.
*   **Platinum:** 2x points multiplier + Free Scheduled Delivery on all bulky items.

# Booking Installation — What to Expect (HH-62)

## 1. Overview
At **HomeHaven**, we provide professional installation services for complex household appliances and smart-home essentials. This guide outlines the booking process and the technical prerequisites required to ensure a successful first-time setup by our certified technicians.

## 2. Booking Process
*   **Checkout Selection:** Installation services can be added directly to your cart for eligible items (e.g., built-in appliances, smart locks, or wall-mounted systems).
*   **Scheduling:** Once your order is confirmed, our Coordination Team will contact you within **24 hours** to align your installation slot with your delivery window.
*   **Combined Service:** For bulky items, we aim for "Deliver & Install" on the same visit to minimize disruption to your schedule.

## 3. Mandatory Site Requirements
To avoid installation failure, the following must be ready prior to the technician's arrival:
*   **Access & Permits:** Ensure the technician has building entry permission and access to the service elevator.
*   **Utility Readiness (Appliances):** 
    *   **Dishwashers/Washers:** Active water inlet (threaded valve), dedicated drainage pipe, and a grounded EU-standard power socket within 1.5 meters.
    *   **Ovens/Hobs:** Proper cabinetry cut-outs (refer to the product’s spec sheet) and high-load electrical wiring.
*   **Infrastructure (Smart Home):**
    *   **Smart Locks:** Compatible door thickness (35mm–55mm) and existing mortise compatibility.
    *   **Thermostats:** Active C-Wire or 24V interface as specified in **HH-58**.
*   **Connectivity:** For smart devices, ensure your **2.4GHz Wi-Fi** password is available for the initial pairing and testing phase.

## 4. Standard Installation Fees
Fees cover labor, basic mounting hardware, and post-install testing. Custom modifications (e.g., cutting granite or rewiring walls) are not included.
*   **Dishwasher (DW60 Series):** AED 149
*   **Washing Machine (WM Series):** AED 129
*   **Smart Door Lock (SN-L2):** AED 99
*   **Smart Thermostat (CC-T1):** AED 79

## 5. Failed Appointment & Revisit Policy
If the technician arrives and the site requirements (utilities, access, or compatibility) are not met:
*   **Postponement:** The job will be logged as "Incomplete."
*   **Revisit Fee:** A flat **AED 75** charge will apply for the second appointment once the site issues are rectified.
*   **Scope Limitation:** Our technicians are not licensed plumbers or structural electricians; they cannot install new water lines or main circuit breakers.

# Installation Day — Common Questions (HH-63)

## 1. Overview
To ensure a smooth service experience, this FAQ clarifies the scope of HomeHaven’s professional installation services. Our technicians are specialized in product integration and testing but operate within strict "Standard Installation" boundaries to ensure safety and compliance with UAE residential codes.

## 2. Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do you perform carpentry or new plumbing for dishwasher/washing machine installs?**  
A: **No.** Standard installation (e.g., for the DW60) includes connecting the unit to your existing water inlet, drain point, and power socket. It does **not** include:
*   Cutting or modifying kitchen cabinetry/granite countertops.
*   Extending plumbing pipes or installing new drainage traps.
*   Running new electrical conduits or installing new breakers.

**Q: Can you fix my door if the smart lock installation leaves marks or holes from the previous lock?**  
A: **No.** Our service covers the mechanical fitment and functional testing of the smart lock (e.g., SN-L2). Any cosmetic repairs, wood filling, or painting required to cover marks left by your old hardware are the responsibility of the homeowner.

**Q: Will you set up the Wi-Fi and app configuration for my smart devices?**  
A: **Partial Support.** 
*   **Hardware Setup:** Our technician will ensure the device is powered and functioning mechanically.
*   **Connectivity:** For the **SN-L2 Smart Lock**, the primary connection is via **Bluetooth** for local app access. 
*   **Network Troubleshooting:** While we assist with initial pairing, we do not provide IT support for home mesh networks, firewall configurations, or ISP-related connectivity issues.

**Q: What if my existing valves are leaking or the power socket is faulty?**  
A: For safety reasons, our technicians cannot proceed with the installation if the site infrastructure is compromised. You will be advised to contact a licensed plumber or electrician to repair the utilities before we can reschedule the installation (refer to the **Revisit Fee in HH-62**).

**Q: Does the installation include removing my old appliance?**  
A: **Disconnect Only.** We will disconnect your old unit to make space for the new one. However, "Haul-Away" or disposal services must be booked separately as a "Recycling Add-on" at checkout to ensure environmentally friendly disposal.

## 3. Post-Installation Sign-off
Upon completion, the technician will demonstrate the basic functions of the device. You will be asked to sign a **Digital Completion Certificate** confirming:
1.  The unit is level and stable.
2.  There are no visible leaks (for appliances).
3.  The locking mechanism functions correctly (for smart locks).
4.  The work area has been cleared of packaging debris.

# Packaging, Unboxing, and Proof of Damage Checklist (HH-21)

## 1. Overview
At **HomeHaven**, we utilize high-durability packaging, but transit incidents can occur. This checklist ensures you have the necessary documentation to support a swift replacement or refund claim. Following these steps protects your consumer rights under our **Support Escalation Policy (HH-66)**.

## 2. Evidence Collection (The 5-Step Photo Rule)
If you suspect your item has been mishandled during transit, do not discard any materials. Capture the following five points of evidence before moving the product:

1.  **Outer Box:** Photograph all six sides of the exterior carton, focusing on crushed corners, punctures, or water spots.
2.  **Shipping Label:** A clear, legible photo of the carrier label showing your **Order ID** and **Tracking Number**.
3.  **Inner Packaging:** Document how the item was secured (e.g., styrofoam, bubble wrap, or cardboard inserts) and if these internal protectors were compromised.
4.  **Product Damage:** Close-up shots of the specific defect (e.g., a cracked **PureBlend PB-900** pitcher or a dented **CrispWave** chassis).
5.  **Retention:** You **must** keep all original packaging and the damaged unit until the claim is officially resolved and a pickup is scheduled.

## 3. Mandatory Reporting Timelines
Claims submitted outside of these windows may be rejected by our logistics insurers:

*   **Damaged Items:** Must be reported within **24 hours** of the delivery timestamp.
*   **Missing Items / Shortages:** If your order contains multiple boxes and one is missing, report it within **48 hours** of the final delivery confirmation.
*   **Wrong Item Received:** Report within **48 hours**; do not open the internal product seal if the outer box indicates the incorrect model.

## 4. Packaging Specifics
*   **“As-Is” Shipping:** To reduce environmental waste, some large-scale appliances (like the **AirPure AP-400**) ship in their original manufacturer’s branded box without an additional outer brown carton. In these cases, the manufacturer’s box acts as the primary shipping container.
*   **Security Seals:** If the "HomeHaven Tamper-Evident Tape" appears cut or resealed upon arrival, document this immediately before opening.

## 5. Filing the Claim
Submit your evidence package via the **HomeHaven App** under "Returns & Support" or email `claims@homehaven.ae` with your **Order ID** in the subject line.

# Inventory Management — Stock Replenishment Rules (HH-80)

## 1. Overview
Efficient inventory flow is the backbone of **HomeHaven’s** commitment to "Next-Day Delivery." These guidelines ensure that high-demand household essentials—from the **CrispWave** air fryers to **AirPure** filter packs—remain in stock across our Al Quoz hub and retail Experience Centers.

## 2. Stock Level Optimization
To prevent "Out of Stock" (OOS) statuses during peak UAE shopping seasons (e.g., Ramadan or White Friday), the following thresholds are enforced:
*   **Minimum Safety Stock:** Each SKU carries a safety buffer calculated as 15% of the average monthly velocity.
*   **Reorder Points (ROP):** Automated triggers in the ERP system initiate a replenishment request when stock hits the ROP, accounting for the **Lead Time** of the specific supplier.
*   **Seasonal Buffers:** Inventory targets for cleaning solutions (e.g., **SteamShine** refills) are increased by 25% during the transition into the summer months.

## 3. Supplier & Procurement Standards
*   **Lead Time Management:** Procurement teams must maintain a verified Lead Time Log for all international and local vendors. 
*   **Minimum Order Quantity (MOQ):** Orders are optimized to meet vendor MOQs while balancing the storage capacity of the Al Quoz warehouse.
*   **Quality Audits:** 5% of every incoming shipment (e.g., a new batch of **PB-900** blenders) undergoes a "Spot-Check" for box integrity, plug compatibility (UAE 3-pin standard), and serial number accuracy.

## 4. Inventory Accuracy & Stock Takes
*   **Quarterly Cycle Counts:** A full wall-to-wall audit is conducted every 90 days. High-value electronics, such as the **SN-L2 Smart Lock**, are subject to monthly "High-Velocity" spot counts.
*   **Variance Investigation:** Any discrepancy exceeding 0.5% between physical stock and the digital ledger triggers an immediate "Root-Cause Analysis" (RCA) to identify potential shrinkage, scanning errors, or fulfillment leakage.
*   **System Sync:** Stock levels displayed on the **HomeHaven App** must synchronize with the warehouse management system (WMS) every 5 minutes to prevent "Ghost Orders."

## 5. Overstock & Obsolescence
*   **Slow-Mover Protocol:** Items with zero sales velocity for 120 days are flagged for the **HavenPlus** "Member-Only Flash Sale" or transferred to the Sharjah "Outlet" location.
*   **Expiry Management:** Consumables (cleaning chemicals/sprays) follow a **First-In, First-Out (FIFO)** picking logic to ensure customers receive products with maximum shelf life.

# Incident Response — Security Breach Protocols (HH-83)

## 1. Overview
This guideline defines the mandatory rapid-response actions required to protect HomeHaven customer data and digital infrastructure in the event of a suspected or confirmed security breach. Our priority is the containment of threats and the transparent protection of our UAE user base.

## 2. Immediate Containment & Actions
Upon detection of a security anomaly (e.g., unauthorized access to the **HavenPlus** database or a brute-force attack on **SN-L2** cloud logs):
*   **Isolate & Contain:** Immediately revoke affected API keys, disable compromised administrative credentials, and segment the impacted network layer to prevent lateral movement.
*   **Evidence Preservation:** Snapshots of volatile memory and system logs must be taken before any reboot or re-imaging. All forensic data must be stored in a write-once-read-many (WORM) environment.
*   **Internal Notification:** Trigger the **Cybersecurity Incident Team (CSIT)** alert. The Data Protection Officer (DPO) and CTO must be briefed within **2 hours** of discovery.

## 3. Regulatory & Legal Obligations
*   **Authority Notification:** In accordance with UAE Federal Data Protection laws, HomeHaven will notify relevant authorities (such as the UAE UAE Cybersecurity Council or TDRA) if the breach poses a high risk to the rights and freedoms of individuals.
*   **Legal Counsel:** All external-facing statements must be reviewed by the Legal Department to ensure compliance with mandatory disclosure timelines.

## 4. Communication Plan
If customer data (e.g., email, physical address, or hashed passwords) is compromised:
*   **Direct Notification:** Affected users will be notified via verified HomeHaven email and in-app alerts within **24–72 hours** of the breach being contained.
*   **Remediation Steps:** Notifications must include clear instructions, such as:
    *   Mandatory password resets for the HomeHaven App.
    *   Enabling Two-Factor Authentication (2FA).
    *   Monitoring bank statements for unusual activity (if payment metadata was involved).
*   **Transparency:** A dedicated "Security Update" landing page will be hosted on the website to provide real-time status reports and FAQ support.

## 5. Post-Incident Recovery & Evolution
*   **Root-Cause Analysis (RCA):** Within 10 business days, a comprehensive forensic report must identify the vulnerability (e.g., unpatched software or a phishing entry point).
*   **Control Updates:** Implement permanent technical fixes, such as enhanced encryption, stricter firewall rules, or hardware-based MFA for staff.
*   **Staff Retraining:** If the breach resulted from human error, all relevant departments must undergo mandatory **Security Awareness Training** within 30 days.
*   **Audit:** An independent third-party security audit will be commissioned to validate the integrity of the restored systems.

## Purpose
This document provides best practice guidelines for securing payment card data in compliance with the **Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI DSS)**. It is intended for retail operations, including in-store and online payment processing systems, to minimize risk of data breaches and ensure customer trust.

---

## Scope
This policy applies to:

- All payment card processing systems (POS terminals, online gateways)
- Databases and applications storing or transmitting cardholder data
- Network and communication channels used for payment transactions
- Employees, contractors, and third-party service providers involved in payment processing

---

## Objectives
- Protect cardholder data from unauthorized access.
- Comply with PCI DSS requirements.
- Minimize the risk of financial fraud and data breaches.
- Establish processes for monitoring, reporting, and responding to security incidents.

---

## Core Payment Security Practices

### 1. Tokenization
- Replace sensitive cardholder data with unique, non-sensitive tokens.
- Ensure tokens cannot be reverse-engineered to reveal the original card data.
- Use tokens consistently in internal systems instead of storing actual card numbers.

### 2. Encryption
- Encrypt cardholder data at rest and in transit using strong encryption standards (e.g., AES-256).
- Implement secure key management practices to protect encryption keys.
- Ensure data is encrypted end-to-end from the point of capture to the processing endpoint.

### 3. Secure Storage
- Avoid storing sensitive authentication data (e.g., full magnetic stripe, CVV) unless absolutely necessary.
- Use secure, access-controlled storage for permitted cardholder information.
- Regularly review stored data for compliance with retention policies and PCI DSS rules.

### 4. Network Security
- Use firewalls, intrusion detection systems (IDS), and intrusion prevention systems (IPS) to protect payment networks.
- Segment payment processing systems from general corporate networks where possible.
- Monitor and log access to payment systems continuously.

### 5. Access Control
- Grant access to cardholder data on a need-to-know basis only.
- Require strong authentication for all employees accessing payment systems.
- Regularly review and update access permissions.

### 6. Security Reviews and Audits
- Conduct regular internal and external security assessments.
- Perform vulnerability scanning and penetration testing according to PCI DSS requirements.
- Document and track remediation of identified vulnerabilities.

### 7. Incident Reporting and Response
- Establish a clear procedure for reporting suspected or confirmed data breaches.
- Ensure rapid containment, investigation, and reporting to relevant authorities.
- Maintain an incident log for audit and compliance purposes.

### 8. Employee Training
- Educate employees on PCI DSS requirements and best practices.
- Train staff to recognize phishing attempts and social engineering risks.
- Reinforce secure handling of payment card data at all times.

---

## Monitoring and Maintenance
- Continuously monitor systems handling cardholder data.
- Update software, firmware, and security tools regularly.
- Review policies and procedures at least annually or after major changes in payment systems.

---

## Compliance
Adherence to this policy ensures alignment with PCI DSS requirements, reduces the risk of payment data breaches, and protects customer trust and company reputation.

## 1. Purpose
The Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) is a mandatory process designed to identify, analyze, and minimize the data privacy risks associated with new projects, technologies, or organizational processes. This ensures "Privacy by Design" is integrated into every home retail initiative.

## 2. Trigger Criteria
A DPIA must be conducted prior to the launch of any project that involves:
New Technologies: Implementing AI, biometric scanning (e.g., in-store heat mapping), or advanced tracking pixels.
Large-Scale Processing: Systematic monitoring of customer behavior or processing data for a significant portion of the customer base.
Sensitive Data: Handling "Special Category" data, including financial details, precise geolocation, or minor-related information.
Third-Party Sharing: Integrating new SaaS vendors or data aggregators that require access to the Customer Master Database.

## 3. Assessment Framework
Every DPIA must document the following four elements:
Description of Processing: A detailed flow of how data is collected, stored, used, and deleted.
Necessity and Proportionality: A justification of why the data is needed and an assessment of whether the project goals can be achieved with less intrusive methods.
Risk Assessment: Identification of potential threats (e.g., data breaches, unauthorized access, or loss of consumer trust) and their likelihood/impact.
Mitigation Measures: Specific technical or organizational safeguards—such as encryption, pseudonymization, or strict access controls—implemented to reduce identified risks.

## 4. Roles and Responsibilities

    *   **Delivery Fees** (Standard or Express).

## 6. Expiry & Technical Issues
*   **Validity:** All codes have a strict expiry date (typically 11:59 PM GST on the date of expiry). 
*   **Support:** If a valid code fails to apply to an eligible **AirPure** or **DustDash** product, please contact **Customer Support (HH-30)** with a screenshot of the error before finalizing your payment.

# Bundle Purchases — Return Handling (HH-43)

## 1. Overview
HomeHaven frequently offers curated bundles—such as the "Smart Kitchen Starter" or "Full-Home Air Quality Pack"—at a discounted rate. Because these items are sold as a single promotional unit, specific return protocols apply to maintain the integrity of the discount provided.

## 2. General Return Policy for Bundles
*   **All-or-Nothing Rule:** To qualify for a full refund, customers must return **all individual components** included in the bundle. This includes any "Free Gifts," accessories, or secondary units (e.g., a spare filter pack included with an **AirPure AP-400**).
*   **Original Condition:** Every item within the bundle must meet the "Shelf-Ready" criteria outlined in **HH-52** (unused, in original packaging, with all seals intact).
*   **Partial Returns:** Requests to return only one item from a bundle (e.g., keeping the blender but returning the toaster) are generally **not permitted**. Any exception to this rule requires manual approval from **Tier 2 Support** and may result in the forfeiture of the original bundle discount.

## 3. Refund Calculation & Logic
*   **Net Paid Amount:** Refunds are calculated based on the actual price paid at checkout after all promotional discounts, bundle-specific markdowns, and **HavenPlus** point redemptions are applied.
*   **Discount Forfeiture:** If a partial return is exceptionally approved, the remaining items you keep will be re-valued at their **Current Individual Retail Price**. The difference will be deducted from your refund total.
*   **Point Reconciliation:** As per **HH-26**, points earned on the entire bundle will be deducted upon return. If points were used to purchase the bundle, they will be restored to your account once the full return is processed.

## 4. Defective Items in a Bundle
*   **Individual Replacement:** If a single component of your bundle is found to be defective (e.g., the **PureBlend** motor fails but the accessories are fine), HomeHaven will replace only the faulty unit. 
*   **Warranty:** The standard 1-year warranty (HH-51) applies to each serialized item within the bundle individually from the date of the original delivery.

## 5. Final Sale Bundles
Bundles marked as "Clearance" or "Final Sale" at the time of purchase are ineligible for change-of-mind returns, though statutory warranty rights for manufacturing defects remain unaffected.

# Buying Guide — Choosing an Air Purifier (HH-45)

## 1. Overview
Indoor air quality in the UAE is impacted by seasonal dust storms, high humidity, and urban particulate matter. Selecting the right air purifier for your HomeHaven household requires balancing filtration efficiency with the specific volume of the room to ensure consistent, clean airflow.

## 2. Core Considerations

### Room Size Coverage (m²)
*   **The "Air Change" Rule:** For optimal health benefits, a purifier should be able to exchange the air in a room 4–5 times per hour. 
*   **Sizing:** Always match the unit’s rated capacity to your largest open area. Using a small-room purifier in a large living hall will result in poor filtration and motor strain.

### Filtration Technology
*   **HEPA-Style (H13):** Mandatory for capturing 99.97% of microscopic desert dust, pollen, and pet dander.
*   **Activated Carbon:** Essential for UAE kitchens and pet-owning homes to neutralize cooking odors, tobacco smoke, and Volatile Organic Compounds (VOCs).
*   **Pre-Filters:** A washable outer layer that traps hair and large lint, extending the life of the more expensive HEPA core.

### Noise Levels & Placement
*   **Bedrooms:** Look for units with a "Sleep Mode" or noise ratings below 30dB. 
*   **Placement:** Ensure the unit has at least 30cm of clearance on all sides. Placing a purifier against a wall or behind a sofa significantly reduces its intake efficiency.

### Maintenance & Operating Costs
*   **Filter Availability:** Always verify that replacement filters (e.g., **AP-F400**) are readily available in-store or online.
*   **Replacement Frequency:** In the UAE, filters typically last 6–8 months. Budget for at least two filter changes per year to maintain the manufacturer's warranty.

## 3. HomeHaven Featured Model: AirPure AP-400
The AP-400 is our most versatile unit, balanced for the typical UAE apartment or villa bedroom.

*   **Coverage:** Up to **35 m²** (Ideal for master bedrooms or home offices).
*   **3-Stage Filtration:** Includes a primary mesh, H13 HEPA-style core, and high-density carbon layer.
*   **Smart Features:**

# Warranty Note — Batteries, Filters, and Wear Items (HH-51)

## 1. Overview
This reference guide clarifies the distinction between manufacturing defects and "Consumable Components" or "Wear Items." While HomeHaven products are built for durability, certain parts are designed to be replaced periodically to maintain peak performance.

## 2. Classification of Wear Items
The following components are subject to natural degradation based on usage frequency and environmental factors. These are generally excluded from standard long-term warranty coverage unless a defect is present at the time of purchase:

*   **Filtration Systems:** HEPA filters, foam pre-filters, and carbon odor filters. These become clogged over time and require replacement to prevent motor strain.
*   **Mechanical Agitators:** Vacuum brush rolls, side brushes, and floor nozzles. Bristles naturally wear down or lose stiffness through friction with floor surfaces.
*   **Energy Storage (Batteries):** Lithium-ion and NiMH batteries. A gradual decline in runtime (capacity) is a chemical characteristic of the technology and is not considered a manufacturing defect unless the battery fails entirely within the first 6 months.
*   **Contact Surfaces:** Rubber seals, gaskets, and non-stick coatings. These may degrade due to heat exposure, cleaning chemicals, or mechanical abrasion.

## 3. Warranty Coverage vs. Maintenance

### What is Covered
*   **Pre-existing Defects:** A battery that does not hold a charge out of the box.
*   **Structural Failure:** A brush roll housing that snaps due to a plastic molding flaw.
*   **Electronic Malfunction:** A filter-monitoring sensor that fails to detect a new filter.

### What is Not Covered
*   **Reduced Runtime:** A vacuum battery that lasts 15 minutes instead of 20 after one year of daily use.
*   **Odors & Clogs:** Filters that have reached their dust capacity or developed odors due to moisture.
*   **Friction Wear:** Smooth or thinning bristles on a cleaning brush after regular use on abrasive surfaces.

## 4. Maintenance Obligations
To keep the primary product warranty (on motors or structural frames) valid, customers must:
1.  **Use Genuine Parts:** Only use HomeHaven-certified filters and batteries. Use of third-party consumables that damage the main unit will void the overall warranty.
2.  **Regular Cleaning:** Clear hair and debris from brush rolls weekly to prevent bearing seize.
3.  **Proper Storage:** Store battery-operated devices in a cool, dry place; extreme heat (e.g., inside a car in the UAE summer) accelerates battery degradation and is considered "misuse."

# VAT & Taxation — UAE Scenario Deep Dive (HH-79)

## 1. Overview
In accordance with the UAE Federal Tax Authority (FTA) Decree-Law No. 8 of 2017, **HomeHaven** operates under a transparent "VAT-Inclusive" pricing model. This reference provides the technical breakdown of tax application for retail sales, service fees, and post-purchase reversals.

## 2. VAT Handling & Display
*   **Retail Standard:** As per **HH-44**, all consumer-facing prices for hardware (e.g., **PureBlend PB-900** or **SecureNest SN-L2**) include the standard 5% VAT. 
*   **Service Fees:** VAT is also applied to and included in the quoted prices for:
    *   **Scheduled Installation:** The AED 149 fee for dishwashers includes AED 7.10 in VAT.
    *   **Express Delivery:** The AED 35 premium shipping fee includes AED 1.67 in VAT.
    *   **COD Fees:** The AED 15 convenience charge is a taxable service.

## 3. Digital Tax Invoicing
*   **Automatic Issuance:** A valid **Tax Invoice** is generated at the moment of "Order Confirmation" and dispatched via email.
*   **Mandatory Fields:** Every HomeHaven invoice includes:
    *   The HomeHaven **Tax Registration Number (TRN)**.
    *   A unique invoice reference number and date.
    *   A line-item breakdown showing the Net Price, VAT Amount (5%), and Gross Total.
*   **Billing Accuracy:** For **Cash on Delivery (COD)**, customers must ensure their "Full Legal Name" is used during checkout to ensure the invoice remains valid for potential corporate expense claims.

## 4. Returns & VAT Adjustments
When a product is returned under the **30-day policy (HH-52)**, a **Tax Credit Note** is issued:
*   **Full Refunds:** The refund amount credited back to your card or bank account includes the original 5% VAT paid.
*   **Partial Returns:** VAT is recalculated based on the remaining items kept. If a bundle discount is forfeited (HH-43), the tax adjustment will reflect the new individual retail prices.
*   **Restocking Fees:** If a **10% Restocking Fee (HH-86)** is applied, this fee is also considered a taxable service; the VAT for the restock service will be deducted from the total refund.

## 5. Non-Resident & Export Scenarios
*   **Tourist Refunds:** HomeHaven Experience Centers are currently not linked to the "Planet" digital VAT refund system. All in-store purchases are final regarding tax collection.
*   **Diplomatic Exemptions:** Entities with a valid VAT Exemption Certificate from the UAE Ministry of Foreign Affairs must contact **Business Support** prior to purchase to arrange a tax-exclusive pro-forma invoice.

## 6. Record Retention
In compliance with FTA requirements, all Tax Invoices and Credit Notes are digitally archived for **7 years** and remain accessible to the customer through the "My Documents" portal in the HomeHaven App.

## Overview
The Product Catalog Sync API allows systems to retrieve, create, and update product information within the retail product catalog. This API is typically used to synchronize product data between inventory systems, ERP platforms, and e-commerce platforms.

---

## Base URL
/api/v1/catalog


---

## Endpoints

### 1. Get Products
Retrieve a list of products from the catalog.

**Endpoint**

GET /catalog/products

**Query Parameters (Optional)**

| Parameter | Type | Description |
|----------|------|-------------|
| category | string | Filter products by category |
| page | integer | Page number for pagination |
| limit | integer | Number of results per page |

**Example Request**

GET /catalog/products?page=1&limit=50


**Example Response**
```json
{
  "products": [
    {
      "product_id": "P1001",
      "name": "Wooden Coffee Table",
      "category": "Furniture",
      "price": 199.99,
      "stock": 45
    }
  ]
}
```

### 2. Create Product

Add a new product to the catalog.

**Endpoint**

POST /catalog/products

**Request Body**

```json
{
  "product_id": "P1002",
  "name": "Modern Floor Lamp",
  "category": "Lighting",
  "price": 89.99,
  "stock": 120,
  "attributes": {
    "color": "black",
    "material": "metal"
  }
}
```

**Response**

```json
{
  "message": "Product created successfully",
  "product_id": "P1002"
}
```

### 3. Update Product (Full Update)

Update all details of an existing product.

**Endpoint**

PUT /catalog/products/{id}

**Path Parameter**


| Parameter | Description |
|----------|-------------|
|id	| Product unique identifier |

**Example Request**

```json
{
  "name": "Modern Floor Lamp",
  "category": "Lighting",
  "price": 79.99,
  "stock": 100,
  "attributes": {
    "color": "black",
    "material": "steel"
  }
}
```

**Response**

```
{
  "message": "Product updated successfully"
}
```

### 4. Partial Update Product

Update specific product fields.

**Endpoint**

PATCH /catalog/products/{id}

**Example Request**
```
{
  "price": 74.99,
  "stock": 90
}
```

**Response**
```
{
  "message": "Product updated successfully"
}
```

**Data Model**

Product Object

|Field	| Type	| Description|
|------|----|----|
|product_id	| string | Unique identifier for the product|
|name	| string	|Product name|
|category|	string|	Product category|
|price|	decimal	|Product selling price|

|attributes|	object	| Additional product attributes (color, size, material, etc.)|
